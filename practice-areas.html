DUI & DWI Defense

California DUI charges can result in jail, license suspension, steep fines, and years on your record. But an arrest is not a conviction — many DUI cases have significant vulnerabilities.

We challenge every aspect of the prosecution's case: the traffic stop, the field sobriety test, the breathalyzer calibration, the blood draw procedure, and more.

  • First, second, and multiple DUI offenses
  • DUI with injury (felony)
  • Underage DUI (Zero Tolerance)
  • Drug DUI (marijuana, prescription)
  • DMV Administrative Hearings
Full DUI Defense Info

⚠️ 10-Day Rule

After a DUI arrest in California, you have exactly 10 calendar days to request a DMV hearing or your license is automatically suspended — even if you're found not guilty.

Call us the moment you're released. We handle the DMV hearing and your criminal case simultaneously.

Drug Charges

Drug charges range from simple possession to possession for sale, transportation, and manufacturing. Each carries drastically different penalties — and each has specific legal vulnerabilities we aggressively pursue.

  • Simple possession (Health & Safety Code 11350)
  • Possession for sale (HS 11351)
  • Drug transportation / distribution
  • Manufacturing & cultivation
  • Drug diversion programs (PC 1000)
  • Challenging unlawful searches & seizures
Free Case Evaluation

Fourth Amendment Defense

Many drug charges stem from searches that violated your constitutional rights. If law enforcement searched your vehicle, home, or person without proper justification, the evidence may be suppressed — and charges dismissed.

Assault & Battery

Assault and battery charges range from simple misdemeanors to aggravated felonies. Even a misdemeanor conviction can impact employment, housing, and professional licensing.

  • Simple assault (PC 240)
  • Battery (PC 242)
  • Aggravated assault / assault with a deadly weapon
  • Assault on a peace officer
  • Self-defense and defense of others

Domestic Violence

Domestic violence charges are among the most aggressively prosecuted crimes in California — often based on one person's account. False and exaggerated allegations do happen, and the consequences of a conviction are severe.

  • PC 273.5 — Corporal injury on spouse/cohabitant
  • PC 243(e) — Domestic battery
  • Restraining / protective order violations
  • Child endangerment related charges
  • Emergency protective order challenges

Expungements & Record Clearing

A criminal record can haunt you long after you've served your sentence. California Penal Code 1203.4 allows many convictions to be expunged — removed from your record so you can move forward with your life.

  • PC 1203.4 expungements
  • Felony reduction to misdemeanor (PC 17(b))
  • Certificate of rehabilitation
  • Juvenile record sealing
  • DACA / immigration-related record considerations
Start Your Expungement

Rebuild Your Future

An expungement allows you to honestly say you have not been convicted of a crime on most job applications and background checks — opening doors to employment, housing, and professional licenses.
