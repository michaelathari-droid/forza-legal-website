California DUI: What You're Facing

A DUI conviction in California carries consequences far beyond a fine. Depending on your prior record and the specifics of the arrest, you could face jail time, license suspension, ignition interlock requirements, DUI school, probation, and a criminal record that follows you for 10 years.

But a DUI arrest is not automatic proof of guilt. Law enforcement must follow strict legal procedures — and many DUI cases have significant weaknesses that an experienced attorney can exploit.

Types of DUI Charges We Defend

First-Offense DUI (VC 23152) The most common charge. Often defensible with the right evidence challenges and negotiation strategy.
DUI with Prior Convictions Second, third, and fourth DUI offenses carry mandatory jail time and enhanced penalties. We fight to minimize consequences.
DUI with Injury (VC 23153) A "wobbler" that can be charged as a felony. Aggressive defense is essential to avoid prison and a felony record.
Felony DUI / DUI Manslaughter The most serious DUI charges. We provide experienced felony defense and work to minimize or defeat all charges.
Underage DUI (VC 23136 / 23140) California's "Zero Tolerance" law applies a 0.01% BAC limit for drivers under 21. We protect young people's futures.
Drug DUI (VC 23152(f)) Driving under the influence of prescription drugs, marijuana, or other substances. Drug recognition evaluations are often unreliable.

The DMV Hearing — Act Within 10 Days

When you're arrested for DUI in California, you have only 10 calendar days to request a DMV Administrative Per Se (APS) hearing to challenge the suspension of your driver's license. If you miss this deadline, your license is automatically suspended — regardless of the outcome of your criminal case.

Forza Legal Defense will request your DMV hearing immediately, fight to preserve your driving privileges, and use the hearing to gather valuable information about the evidence in your criminal case.
