How We Defend You

Criminal prosecution in California is a serious and complex process. From the moment you're arrested or under investigation, the government is building its case against you. You need someone building your defense simultaneously — and building it better.

At Forza Legal Defense, PC, we represent clients at every stage: investigation, arrest, arraignment, preliminary hearings, pre-trial motions, negotiation, trial, sentencing, and appeals.

01
Immediate Response & Arrest Defense

The first 24 hours matter enormously. We respond immediately, advise you on what to say (and not say), and work to minimize bail and pre-trial conditions.

02
Case Investigation & Evidence Review

We obtain police reports, body camera footage, witness statements, and all discovery materials. We look for constitutional violations, contradictions, and weaknesses in the prosecution's case.

03
Pre-Trial Motions

We file motions to suppress illegally obtained evidence, dismiss charges with insufficient basis, and challenge procedural violations — often before trial even begins.

04
Negotiation & Plea Strategy

When negotiating is in your interest, we negotiate from a position of strength — armed with a full understanding of the evidence and the law — to pursue reduced charges or dismissal.

05
Trial Defense

We are experienced trial attorneys who are never afraid to take a case to a jury. When a trial is the right strategy, we are prepared, aggressive, and relentless.

Felony vs. Misdemeanor Charges

California criminal offenses fall into three categories: infractions, misdemeanors, and felonies. Misdemeanors carry up to one year in county jail; felonies can result in years in state prison. Some offenses are "wobblers" — meaning they can be charged as either, depending on the facts and the prosecutor's discretion.

The distinction matters enormously. A felony conviction can strip you of your right to vote, own firearms, and hold professional licenses. We fight to reduce felonies to misdemeanors whenever possible — and to keep misdemeanors off your record.

Your Constitutional Rights

  • Right to remain silent (5th Amendment)
  • Right to an attorney (6th Amendment)
  • Right to a speedy trial
  • Right to confront witnesses
  • Protection from unreasonable searches
  • Presumption of innocence
  • Proof beyond a reasonable doubt

If You're Arrested

1. Stay calm. Do not resist.
2. Invoke your right to remain silent.
3. Ask for an attorney immediately.
4. Do not consent to searches.
